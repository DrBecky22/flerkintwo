Stop Running and let the Flerkin Eat You...

Hi - I'm Dr. Becky Andrews and this is the demo page for my "leaning to code" journey. Yeah, I know, learning to code in your 50's - in 2025 when coding jobs are going away - insanity... It has been said, it has been noted... Sometimes you just have to follow your passion. I am enjoying learning to code every bit as much as I enjoy studying medicine. They are surprisingly similar. I began my self-guided 'learn to code' adventure in March 2022 with my eye on self-building resources needed by my community and patient base. Being a holistic thinker, that has lead me down a long winding path to understand how it all works together. I've completed several self-paced bootcamps, and one "real time" bootcamp that was not very helpful. There has been much eye-rolling from various teachers when I want to take 6 month detours to learn how something really works, but I stick by my process and after 3 years it is finally yeilding results.


I do owe a big shout out to:

  1. My brother - the networking, server, security and history guru - Mike Andrews of fark.com - yes, THAT Mike Andrews
  2. My "pulling it all together" tutor Miguel Ludert
  3. Colt Steele's Fullstack Bootcamp on Udemy - his amazing teaching really helped make sense of it all
  4. Dr. Angela Yu and her Fullstack Bootcamp on Udemy - I didn't finish that camp, but having a British physician teach me basic coding was the spark that got me to believe that this was even possible

What you are seeing here is 100% hand-coded by me. That includes:

  1. Homepage - simple HTML with responsive Tailwind CSS styling
  2. CatGallery - simple HTML with Flexbox and responsive Bootstrap styling
  3. SnuffyGames - static HTML with Bootstrap and Basic Javascript
  4. ToDo and TacoStand are not yet live, they will be tiny MERN full-stack proof-of-concept projects using React, forms, and a database
  5. It is being served to you from a hand-build VM Droplet on Digital Ocean
  6. The server is running Ubuntu, Nginx, MongoDB Community edition, and connecting to Mailgun for updates and error messaging
  7. Domain is hosted at Cloudflare, making use of their security features, and TLS/SSL certificates
  8. Code is deployed from GitHub - someday automated with GitHub actions or webhooks
  9. Yes, I know there are still some style things to fix... keep yer pants on... I'm working on functionality first


Yeah, it was hard. It has taken over 3 years to learn enough to get what you are seeing in front of you and functional... but this was the lynchpin. Next I will learn to use modern 'serverless' deployment on AWS. Then... lookout
